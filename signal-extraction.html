SIGNAL EXTRACTION

Signal extraction plays a pivotal role in bio-signal processing, particularly when working with complex data like ECG (Electrocardiogram) signals. ECG data contains valuable information about the electrical activity of the heart, but it is often accompanied by noise and artifacts from external sources like muscle movements or interference from other electronic devices. To ensure accurate analysis, the raw signals must first be preprocessed. In our system, we use advanced filtering techniques to remove noise and enhance the clarity of the ECG signal. Once cleaned, we focus on extracting key features such as heart rate variability (HRV), R-peaks, QRS complexes, and intervals between heartbeats. These features are crucial indicators of heart function and can reveal potential irregularities or abnormalities. The extracted signals are then fed into our machine learning model, which processes the data to make heart rate predictions and assess the risk of heart attacks. Accurate signal extraction not only improves the model's performance but also ensures that we can reliably monitor cardiovascular health, offering users a clear picture of their heart's condition. By refining this process, we aim to provide an efficient and non-invasive tool for early detection and prevention of heart-related issues.
