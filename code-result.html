CODE

Our heart rate prediction and heart attack risk assessment project utilizes several machine learning algorithms to analyze real-time ECG data effectively. Logistic Regression is one of the primary algorithms used, enabling us to predict the probability of heart attack risks based on extracted features like heart rate variability, age, and sex. This method is well-suited for binary classification, helping us determine whether an individual is at risk. We also implement Support Vector Machines (SVM), which are effective for classifying ECG data into normal and abnormal categories. SVMs excel in high-dimensional spaces, making them ideal for the complex nature of ECG signals. Another critical component is Random Forest, an ensemble learning method that constructs multiple decision trees to enhance prediction accuracy. This algorithm improves robustness and reduces the risk of overfitting, making it reliable in handling noisy ECG data. Additionally, we utilize K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) to classify heart rate patterns by comparing current data with historical cases. KNN helps identify similarities and aids in predicting potential heart conditions. Together, these algorithms provide a comprehensive framework for accurate and timely heart risk assessments, ensuring that users receive reliable insights into their cardiovascular health.
